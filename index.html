Crabs are remarkable crustaceans found in oceans and coastal areas around the world. They belong to the order Decapoda and are known for their distinctive sideways walk and hard exoskeletons.

General Information

Scientific Classification: Crabs belong to the phylum Arthropoda and class Crustacea. There are approximately 7,000 species of crabs worldwide!

Characteristics

  • Ten Legs: Crabs have 10 legs - 8 for walking and 2 larger claws called chelipeds
  • Exoskeleton: Their hard outer shell provides protection
  • Eyes: Located on stalks called eyestalks that can move independently
  • Sideways Walk: The unique arrangement of their legs allows them to walk sideways efficiently
  • Colors: Crabs come in various colors including red, blue, brown, and orange

Habitat and Diet

Crabs live in various marine environments including rocky shores, sandy beaches, coral reefs, and the deep sea. They are omnivores and scavengers, feeding on algae, worms, mollusks, and other organic matter. Some crabs are skilled hunters of small fish and invertebrates.

Reproduction

Crabs reproduce sexually, and many species migrate to deeper waters to spawn. Female crabs can lay thousands of eggs at once, which develop into larvae before settling on the seafloor and growing into adult crabs.
