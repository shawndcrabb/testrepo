Hermit Crab

A unique crab that doesn't have a hard shell of its own. They must find and carry shells for protection throughout their lives.

Size: 0.5-20 inches
Habitat: Tropical waters worldwide
Special: Changes shells as it grows

Japanese Spider Crab

The largest crab species in the world! With long, spindly legs that can span up to 12 feet.

Leg Span: Up to 12 feet
Habitat: Deep waters of Japan
Lifespan: Over 100 years

Horseshoe Crab

Ancient species that has existed for over 445 million years. Despite the name, they're not true crabs but chelicerates.

Size: 15-24 inches across
Age: One of Earth's oldest creatures
Medical: Blood used for medical testing

Fiddler Crab

Named for their one large claw that resembles a violin. Males wave their claw to attract females.

Size: 1-2 inches
Habitat: Salt marshes and beaches
Behavior: Males perform mating displays

Mr. Krabs

The famous money-loving owner of the Krusty Krab restaurant. A fictional character from the animated series SpongeBob SquarePants known for his love of money and successful business ventures.

Species: Anthropomorphic Crab
Habitat: Bikini Bottom
Special Trait: Obsessed with money and profit!

Why Crabs Are Fascinating

Crabs have evolved into thousands of different species, each adapted to their specific environments. From the depths of the ocean to shallow tide pools, crabs demonstrate incredible diversity in size, color, behavior, and habitat preferences. Some are tiny enough to fit on your fingernail, while others can span across a room! This remarkable variety makes crabs one of the most successful and adaptable groups of creatures in our oceans.
